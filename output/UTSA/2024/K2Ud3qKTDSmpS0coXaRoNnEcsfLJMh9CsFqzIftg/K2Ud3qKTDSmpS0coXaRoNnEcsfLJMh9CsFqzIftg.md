# 2024 UTSA Roadrunners Football UTSA vs. Rice Saturday, Oct.12 Rice Stadium·Houston, Texas UTSA Postgame Notes

## TEAM RECORDS AND SERIES NOTES

- UTSA fell to 2-4 overall and 0-2 in American Athletic Conference play. Rice improved to 2-4 and 1-2.

- This marked the 12th meeting between UTSA and Rice.

The Roadrunners lead the all-time series, 8-4.

- Head coach Jeff Traylor has surpassed Larry Coker for the most games coached in program history with his 59th today.

- In the Jeff Traylor era, UTSA is now:

41-18 (.695) overall.

27-6 (.818) in regular-season conference games.

29-6 (.829) versus conference competition when including the 2021 and 2022 Conference USA Championship Games.

## TEAM NOTES

- UTSA held Rice to 389 total yards (42 rushing / 347 passing).

The Roadrunners have limited their last three opponents to a combined 139 rushing yards (46.3 ypg).

UTSA now has held four opponents to 51 or fewer yards on the ground.

- The Roadrunners had a fumble recovery in the first quarter and now have a takeaway in 16 consecutive games.

- UTSA now has recorded a sack in 16 straight contests after Jimmori Robinson dropped Rice quarterback E.J. Warner in the fourth quarter.

- UTSA is now 22-11 in games decided by one score, and 20 of the last 37 contests have been decided in the fourth quarter or later.

## INDIVIDUAL NOTES

- Senior ILB Martavius French posted seven total tackles including two for loss.

o This marks back-to-back games that French has led the team in tackles and the third game overall.

○ French also recorded a pass breakup and a QB hurry.

- Junior CB Zah Frazier forced a fumble in the first quarter that was recovered by Elliott Davison to extend UTSA's takeaway streak to 16 games.

- Senior $B Donyai Taylor and redshirt sophomore Owen Pewee each tallied five tackles.

- Redshirt sophomore QB Owen McCown completed 28 passes for 256 yards and three touchdowns, matching his career-best completions.

He added 53 yards on the ground for a new career high and his 23-yard scamper was the longest rush of the day.

This marks the third time in McCown's career that he has thrown three touchdown passes.

- Sophomore WR Devin McCuin had 104 all-purpose yards (83 receiving /21 rushing) with a six-yard touchdown reception in the fourth quarter.

His 40-yard reception in the fourth quarter is a season long.

With seven catches, it is the fifth game McCuin has led UTSA in receptions and brings his season total to 37 for 279 yards.

- Redshirt sophomore TE Houston Thomas caught two passes for 43 yards and a score that gave UTSA the lead with 2:06 remaining.

- His 38-yard touchdown reception was the first of his career.

- Sophomore WR DJ Allen caught three passes for 38 yards and a touchdown.

He scored his first career touchdown on a 20-yard reception in the second quarter.

He also hauled in six receptions for 36 yards.

- Sophomore PK Tate Sandell was 2-for-2 on field goals and made three extra-point kicks.

- Senior WR Chris Carpenter had a 43-yard kickoff return in the third quarter, UTSA's longest kick return of the season.

He connected from 46 yards in the first half and then made a 52-yarder in the third quarter.

His 52-yard FG is the second-longest in program history, one yard shy of the record shared by Chase Allen and Sean Ianno.

- Junior P Caile Hogan averaged 46 yards on four punts.

He unleashed a 54-yard punt in the third quarter to match his longest punt of the season.
