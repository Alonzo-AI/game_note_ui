# 2025 UTSA Roadrunners Football UTSA 57, FIU 20 (First Responder Bowl) Friday, Dec. 26 Gerald J. Ford Stadium Dallas, Texas UTSA Postgame Notes

- UTSA finished the season at 7-6 overall. FIU fell to 7-6.

This marked the fifth meeting between the two schools and UTSA now leads the series, 3-2

- Tonight's appearance in the SERVPRO First Responder Bowl was UTSA's sixth straight and seventh overall bowl game.

UTSA made its second showing in the First Responder Bowl (L, 24-31 vs. Louisiana, 2020).

The Roadrunners are now 3-4 in bowl games with their third straight bowl victory.

- The Roadrunners played nine teams that made a bowl game in 2025, finishing with a 4-5 record in those contests.

- UTSA finished the 2025 season with a 4-2 record in night games.

- UTSA is now 2-3 all-time in neutral-site games.

- UTSA is 14-8 (.636) in weekday games including 10-4 (.714) on Fridays.

- Head coach Jeff Traylor is now 53-26 (.671) overall (since 2020).

He is 3-3 in bowl games at UTSA.

He is 12-5 (.706) on weekdays including 9-3 (.750) on Fridays.

## TEAM NOTES

- UTSA scored a program bowl-record 57 points, the third-most points scored in a game in school history.

The 31 points in the first half also are a school record for a bowl game.

UTSA scored 50 or more points for the third time this season.

UTSA has scored 40-or-more points in two straight bowl games.

UTSA has scored 20-plus points in 21 of the last 23 games.

UTSA now has surpassed 20 first-half points in three consecutive bowl games.

- The Roadrunners racked up seven total touchdowns, including five rushing and three passing TDs, all program records for a bowl game.

- UTSA piled up 481 total yards, including a school bowl-record 295 passing, with 186 more coming on the ground.

The Roadrunners have recorded 400-plus yards of offense in 13 of their last 18 games.

o UTSA has registered 400-or-more yards in back-to-back bowl games.

- UTSA held FIU to 255 yards of offense (176 passing and 79 rushing) and to 1-of-10 fourth-down conversions.

- UTSA posted eight tackles for loss and three sacks.

The Roadrunners have a sack in 35 of their last 36 games.

- UTSA came up with a pair of interceptions and now has recorded a takeaway in 32 of its last 36 games.

- The Roadrunners racked up 10 quarterback hurries, the second-most in a game in program history and the most in a bowl.

## INDIVIDUAL NOTES

- Junior QB Owen McCown was named First Responder Bowl MVP after connecting on 18-of-28 passes for 296 yards and three touchdowns.

McCown has been named MVP of UTSA's bowl game in back-to-back seasons and is now 3-0 as the starting quarterback in the postseason.

He finished the season with 2,995 yards,30 passing TDs and a 149.7 passing efficiency rating on 277-of-410 passing (.676).

This year's totals put him second in passing TDs, third in passing efficiency, attempts and completions and fourth in passing yards in the school's single-season record book.

- Redshirt freshman RB Will Henderson III rushed for a team-high 59 yards and two touchdowns, while also charting a 40-yard touchdown reception.

Henderson set a program bowl game record with three total touchdowns and matched the standards for rushing TDs (2) and receiving TDs (1).

- Junior WR Devin McCuin caught six passes for 73 yards and a score.

McCuin now has a reception in 31 consecutive games.

He tied two other Roadrunners for fourth place on the school's single-season touchdown receptions chart with his eighth and his 16 career receiving TDs are tied for fourth.

He moved into eighth place on UTSA's single-season list with 726 receiving yards and into third place with 1,696 career receiving yards.

- Redshirt sophomore TE Patrick Overmver hauled in a pair of passes for 50 yards and a score.

- Junior RB Bryson Donnell tallied 51 yards on seven carries with a touchdown.

- Sophomore RB A'Marion Peterson punched in a TD and 28 yards on eight carries.

- Senior ILB Shad Banks Jr. recorded eight total tackles, a sack, two quarterback hurries and a pass breakup.

- Junior $B Owen Pewee had three tackles, a pass breakup and a pair of hurries.

- Sophomore S Jimmy Wyrick recorded an interception.

- Sophomore CB Ahamad Chapman had three tackles, a pass breakup and a 2-point-conversion interception return for a pair of points.

That marked the second-ever defensive extra point for UTSA and the first since Teddrick McGhee returned a blocked extra point kick attempt against Old Dominion on Sept. 24, 2016.

- Senior S Tyan Milton had four tackles, an interception and a pass breakup.

- Redshirt freshman ILB James Walley Jr. had four tackles, a sack and 1.5 TFLs.

- Redshirt senior DL Jon Jones recorded a sack, a pass breakup and a quarterback hurry.

- Senior P Caile Hogan had four punts for 164 yards, an average of 41.0 yards per punt.

He ranks second on UTSA's single-season list with a 45.78 punting average, just behind the record of 45.98 by Lucas Dean in 2020.

- Sophomore PK Michael Petro booted a pair of field goals and seven PATs.

His seven extra points on the evening represent a school bowl record.

He finished the season with 17 field goals, good for fifth on the program's single-season list.

He was 56-of-56 on PATs, the third-most made in a single season, and his 107 points are the third-most.

## ADDITIONAL NOTES

- UTSA's captains today were senior ILB Shad Banks Jr., senior OL Cory Godinet, senior DL Jon Jones and sophomore S Jimmy Wyrick.

- The Roadrunners wore blue helmets, blue jerseys and blue pants, and the record now stands at 13-7 (.650) in that uniform combination
