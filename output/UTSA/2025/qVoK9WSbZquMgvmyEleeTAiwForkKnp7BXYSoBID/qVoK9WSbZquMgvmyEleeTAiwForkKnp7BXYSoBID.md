## TEAM RECORDS AND SERIES NOTES

- UTSA improved to 3-3 overall and 1-1 in American Conference play. Rice fell to 3-4 and 1-3.

The Roadrunners lead the series, 9-4, including 6-1 at home.

This was the 13th meeting, tying (for one week) the North Texas series as the most played in program history.

UTSA is 4-0 versus Rice in Homecoming games.

- The Roadrunners are 11-4 in Homecoming games.

- UTSA is 57-31 (.648) in games played at the Alamodome, including 2-1 this season.

- UTSA is 60-43 (.583) in conference games.

The Roadrunners are 12-6 (.667) in American Conference games.

- UTSA is 39-25 (.609) against teams from the state of Texas.

- The Roadrunners are now 31-18 (.633) against teams that have a bird as their nickname or mascot.

UTSA is now 14-8 (.636) versus teams that use Owls as their nickname.

- Head coach Jeff Traylor is 49-23 (.681) overall (since 2020).

UTSA is 31-4 (.888) at the Alamodome in the Jeff Traylor era.

Traylor is 21-0 in regular season conference home games and 23-0 when including the 2021 and 2022 Conference USA Championship Games, which were played at the Alamodome.

He is 6-0 in Homecoming games.

He is 19-9 (.679) versus teams from the state of Texas.

UTSA has outscored Rice 147-50 in the Jeff Traylor era.

## TEAM NOTES

- UTSA scored a school-record 61 points, breaking the previous mark of 56 vs. Northwestern Oklahoma State on Sept. 22, 2012.

- It marked the 13th time UTSA has broken the 50-point barrier and the first since the 51-27 home win over Temple on Nov 22. 2024.

The Roadrunners now have scored 50-plus points eight times in the Jeff Traylor era (since 2020)

- The Roadrunners scored on their first possession for the second straight game.

- UTSA tallied 38 points in the first half, the most in either half this year.

- UTSA scored 23 points in the third quarter, the highest total for any quarter this season after scoring 21 in the first quarter tonight.

- UTSA tied the program record with three defensive scores, a fumble return for a touchdown, an interception return for a touchdown and a safety, all of which occurred in the third quarter.

The Roadrunners had a safety and two interception returns for a TD in a 25-6 win at UTEP on Oct. 3, 2015.

- UTSA scored two defensive touchdowns in a game for the third time in program history with an interception return by Kendrick Blackshire and a fumble return by Shad Banks Jr.

The last time the Roadrunners had two defensive TDs was a pair of INT returns in the 45-0 win over Rice on Oct.16,2021,and the only other time was also two interception returns in the 25-6 victory at UTEP on Oct.3,2015.

- The safety in the third quarter by Jon Jones was the seventh in program history and first since Joe Evans against Florida Atlantic on Oct. 21,2023.

- The Roadrunners recorded 17 tackles for loss,representing a new program record and the most of any FBS program in a single game this season.

- JTSA had two sacks and now has registered a sack in 28 of the past 29 games.

- UTSA posted 437 total yards of offense, with 246 through the air and 191 on the ground.

- The Roadrunners racked up 315 yards of offense in the first half, their most in a half since registering 360 in the first half of the 51-27 win over Temple on Nov.22, 2024. It also marked the first time UTSA has surpassed 300 yards in a half since the first half of the 2024 Myrtle Beach Bowl win over Coastal Carolina on Dec. 23.

- UTSA held Rice to 269 yards of offense and 79 yards passing,the fewest by any opponent since Southern Miss managed 52 on Nov.13, 2021.
